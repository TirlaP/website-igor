Initial Analysis and Estimation

The restoration process starts with a detailed analysis of existing damage and an estimation of the percentage of the glass area that needs to be repaired.

Removal and Transportation

Removing a stained-glass window from a building requires experience and a careful pair of hands. Once removed from the original location, a stained-glass window is carefully packaged and transported to our workshop.

Documentation and Lead Removal

Before beginning the restoration, the existing condition of the stained glass is photo-recorded and documented. This is followed by the careful removal of the old lead.

Cleaning and Preservation of Original Glass

Next, all stained-glass parts are cleaned thoroughly to preserve as much of the original glass as possible.

Heritage

Repairing and Replacing Damaged Glass

There are many types of damage that can be found on stained-glass windows. Glass with small cracks can be repaired using a special kind of glue or copper foil.

Replacing Severely Damaged Glass

The more damaged sections must be replaced with new, precisely cut glass that matches the original in color and structure.

Painting and Fusing New Glass Pieces

New glass parts must be painted to match the color and design of the damaged original. The paint is created using a special formula that must be heated to fuse with the glass surface.

Learn More

Reassembly and Structural Reinforcement

Releading the Restored Glass Pieces

After restoring all stained-glass parts, the pieces are reassembled using new lead profiles. In some cases, the old lead doesn’t need to be replaced, but it is often better to do so due to the lead’s age, stability, and to prevent potential future damage.

Applying Putty for Stability and Protection

Before the stained glass is completed, a special putty formula is applied between the glass and lead, stabilizing the piece and ensuring it is wind- and waterproof.

Dedication to Unique Craftsmanship

Every stained-glass window is unique due to its style and craftsmanship. Our goal and passion are to approach each restoration case with care, respecting the tradition and history of each stained-glass piece.

Final Steps in Restoration

We document and photo-record the stained-glass window again after all work is complete.

The final step of the restoration process is to place the stained glass back into its original window frame and building.
